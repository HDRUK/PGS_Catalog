What is a PGS?

Polygenic Scores (PGS) are a method to predict an individualâ€™s genetic predisposition to a trait, by aggregating the effects of genomic variants into a single score. PGS are typically comprised of hundreds-to-millions of variants (usually SNPs), combined as a weighted sum of genotype dosages with effect sizes discovered by GWAS.

PGS may also be called genetic, genomic, or polygenic risk scores (GRS / PRS) if they refer to a score that predicts a clinical trait or disease phenotype of interest.

About the PGS Catalog

The PGS Catalog is an open database of published polygenic scores (PGS). Each PGS in the Catalog is consistently annotated with relevant metadata; including scoring files (variants, effect alleles/weights), annotations of how the PGS was developed and applied, and evaluations of their predictive ability.

More about the PGS Catalog project, and a description of our eligibility criteria and data can be found in our documentation.
